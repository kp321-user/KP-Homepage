The Russian Revolution (1917)

The Russian Revolution was a series of political and social upheavals that ended the centuries-old Russian monarchy, led to the rise of the Bolsheviks, and created the Soviet Union.

Background

By the early 20th century, Russia was ruled by an absolute monarchy under Tsar Nicholas II.

Major problems included:

  • Widespread poverty among peasants and workers.
  • Poor working conditions in rapidly growing cities.
  • Weak industrial development compared to Western Europe.
  • Political repression and lack of democratic rights.
  • Military failures, especially in World War I.

Public dissatisfaction had already boiled over once in the Revolution of 1905, which forced limited reforms but did not solve deeper issues.

The February Revolution (1917)

In early 1917, food shortages and war exhaustion led to mass protests in Petrograd (now St. Petersburg).

Key events:

  • Strikes and demonstrations spread across the city.
  • Soldiers refused to fire on protesters and joined them.
  • The government collapsed.

Tsar Nicholas II abdicated the throne, ending over 300 years of Romanov rule.

A Provisional Government was formed, but it struggled to maintain control and continue Russia’s involvement in World War I.

The October Revolution (1917)

Later in 1917, the Bolsheviks, a revolutionary socialist group led by Vladimir Lenin, seized power.

They promised:

  • Peace (end Russia’s involvement in WWI)
  • Land reform for peasants
  • Power to workers’ councils (soviets)

In October, Bolshevik forces took control of key government buildings in Petrograd and overthrew the Provisional Government with relatively little resistance.

This event is known as the October Revolution.

Civil War (1918–1922)

After the Bolsheviks took power, Russia descended into a brutal civil war.

The Two Sides

  • Red Army – Bolsheviks (communists)
  • White Army – a loose alliance of monarchists, liberals, and anti-Bolshevik groups

The war was extremely violent and caused massive suffering among civilians.

The Bolsheviks ultimately won due to:

  • Better organization
  • Control of major cities
  • Strong leadership under Lenin and military commander Leon Trotsky

Creation of the Soviet Union

In 1922, the Bolsheviks formally established the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR).

This marked:

  • The first communist state in the world.
  • The end of the Russian Empire.
  • The beginning of a new political system based on Marxist-Leninist ideology.

Results and Legacy

The Russian Revolution had major global consequences:

  • Ended the rule of the Tsars.
  • Created the Soviet Union, a major world power.
  • Inspired communist movements worldwide.
  • Led to decades of political tension between communist and capitalist countries.
  • Set the stage for much of 20th-century global politics.

In One Sentence

The Russian Revolution was a dramatic collapse of the Tsarist monarchy and the rise of the Bolsheviks, resulting in the creation of the Soviet Union and a major shift in world history.
